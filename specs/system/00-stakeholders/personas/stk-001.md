# Persona: Developer / Operator / Owner / Data Consumer

**Stakeholder ID:** STK-001
**Interest Category:** User + Owner + Maintainer
**Date:** 2026-05-08

## Profile

**Technical Background:** Experienced across the full stack relevant to this system — Python, Docker, MQTT, MariaDB, Linux administration. No significant technical knowledge gaps relative to the system's current scope.

**Typical Interaction with System:** Primarily set-and-forget. The service runs continuously in the background; interaction is triggered by a specific need rather than routine. When inspection is required, uses PyCharm Pro to check container status and run ad-hoc SQL queries against the database directly. No dashboard or analysis tooling exists; direct database queries are the current substitute for analytics.

**Operating Constraints:**
- Time is the primary constraint. The system is maintained alongside full-time work and family obligations; attention arrives in irregular bursts, not continuously.
- When a work window opens, it must be spent productively — not on re-learning context.
- Budget and tooling are not constraints. Open-source tooling is preferred by ecosystem fit, not cost.

---

## Goals

- **Immediate (this summer):** Reliable, gap-free capture of all sensor readings so that the effect of cooling measures on the attic and bedrooms can be evaluated with confidence.
- **Medium term (ongoing):** A trustworthy historical record that reveals seasonal patterns and the impact of renovations or temporary measures on comfort and energy use.
- **Long term:** An intact, growing dataset that future-self can mine for analyses not yet defined. The system runs unattended and continues working without regular intervention.
- **As developer:** A codebase that is legible, extensible, and unsurprising after months away — one that does not break silently or require context reconstruction.

---

## Pain Points

- **Silent failures (critical):** The service has stopped logging in the past without notification; gaps in the record were only discovered retroactively. A system that fails invisibly is worse than one that fails loudly.
- **No observability beyond container status:** The only current health check is `docker compose ps`. No health endpoint, no alerting, no evidence of successful writes.
- **Spurious zero values:** HomeMatic publishes zero values for all readings on startup, including physically impossible ones. These pollute the dataset and are difficult to distinguish from legitimate zero readings.
- **Silent drift:** The system continues running but something upstream changes — a topic is renamed, a sensor stops publishing, a new sensor is added — and the record becomes silently incomplete. Considered the most insidious failure mode.
- **Setup and maintenance friction:** Returning after a long absence requires re-learning how the system works. Lack of documentation and a CHANGELOG amplifies this.

---

## Resistance Factors

- **Data completeness uncertainty:** If gaps, silent failures, or spurious values cannot be detected or accounted for, any analysis becomes suspect. Inability to say "the record is complete for this period" undermines the entire purpose.
- **Unexplained behaviour:** Unexpected restarts, strange log output, or errors with no clear cause — especially after a long absence — erode trust.
- **Schema or data surprises:** Unexpected nulls, wrong units, or missing sensors with no audit trail would be deeply unsettling and difficult to recover from.
- **Unreproducible fixes:** Problems resolved without fully understanding the cause leave doubt about recurrence.

---

## Current Workaround

None exists. There is no alternative system, no manual fallback, and no way to recover readings from a period of outage. When mqttlogger is down, data is permanently lost. The MQTT broker does not buffer historical messages and sensors do not store readings locally. Downtime is directly and irrecoverably destructive to the historical record.
